Weather forecasting is the process of predicting the state of the atmosphere at a specific location and time in the future. It involves analyzing current weather data, such as temperature, humidity, wind speed, and atmospheric pressure, and using mathematical models to predict how these variables will change over time. Weather forecasts are typically issued for a specific period, such as the next few hours, the next day, or the next week. They are used by individuals, businesses, and governments to make informed decisions about a wide range of activities, including agriculture, transportation, construction, and emergency management.
